Esto es súper importante porque muchas veces confundimos cuándo usar cada tiempo verbal.

Exacto. Empecemos con el simple present – presente simple. Usamos el presente simple para hablar de habits – hábitos, routines – rutinas, facts – hechos, y things that are generally true – cosas que son generalmente verdaderas.

Correcto. La estructura es muy sencilla. Con I, you, we, they usamos el verbo en su forma base. I eat vegetables every day – Como verduras todos los días. They drink coffee in the morning – Ellos toman café por la mañana. We cook at home – Cocinamos en casa.

Y con he, she, it agregamos una S al final del verbo. She eats breakfast at seven – Ella desayuna a las siete. He drinks tea – Él toma té. It tastes delicious – Sabe delicioso.

Exacto. Más ejemplos.
  • I usually have cereal for breakfast – Usualmente como cereal para el desayuno
  • My sister prefers toast with jam – Mi hermana prefiere tostada con mermelada
  • We always eat together on Sundays – Siempre comemos juntos los domingos

Ahora, para hacer preguntas en presente simple, usamos do o does.
  • Do you like spicy food? – ¿Te gusta la comida picante?
  • Does he cook often? – ¿Él cocina seguido?
  • Do they eat meat? – ¿Ellos comen carne?

Y para negativo, usamos don't o doesn't.
  • I don't eat fast food – No como comida rápida
  • She doesn't drink soda – Ella no toma refresco
  • They don't like vegetables – A ellos no les gustan las verduras


Perfecto. Ahora hablemos del present continuous – presente continuo. Lo usamos para acciones que están happening now – ocurriendo ahora, o para temporary situations – situaciones temporales.

La estructura es subject + am/is/are + verb with ING.
  • I am cooking dinner right now – Estoy cocinando la cena ahora mismo
  • She is eating chocolate – Ella está comiendo chocolate
  • They are drinking wine – Ellos están tomando vino

Más ejemplos.
  • We are trying to eat healthier these days – Estamos tratando de comer más saludable estos días
  • He is following a special diet this month – Él está siguiendo una dieta especial este mes
  • I am cutting down on sugar right now – Estoy reduciendo el azúcar ahora mismo

Para preguntas, invertimos el sujeto y el verbo to be.
  • Are you eating? – ¿Estás comiendo?
  • Is she cooking? – ¿Ella está cocinando?
  • Are they drinking coffee? – ¿Están tomando café?

Y para negativo, agregamos not después del verbo to be.
  • I am not eating meat today – No estoy comiendo carne hoy
  • She is not drinking alcohol – Ella no está tomando alcohol
  • We are not cooking tonight – No estamos cocinando esta noche
Ahora, aquí viene la parte súper importante. Algunos verbos son action verbs – verbos de acción, y otros son nonaction verbs – verbos de estado. Los verbos de acción los podemos usar en presente simple Y en presente continuo.

Por ejemplo, to eat – comer es un verbo de acción. Podemos decir I eat breakfast every day – Como desayuno todos los días, en presente simple. Y también I am eating breakfast right now – Estoy comiendo desayuno ahora mismo, en presente continuo.

Exacto. Otros verbos de acción son to cook – cocinar, to drink – beber, to run – correr, to work – trabajar, to study – estudiar. Todos estos los podemos usar en ambos tiempos.

Pero los nonaction verbs – verbos de estado son diferentes. Estos verbos normalmente NO los usamos en presente continuo. Solo en presente simple.

Los verbos de estado describen states – estados, no actions – acciones. Por ejemplo, to like – gustar, to love – amar, to hate – odiar, to prefer – preferir, to believe – creer, to know – saber, to understand – entender, to think cuando significa believe – pensar cuando significa creer.

Entonces, decimos I like chocolate – Me gusta el chocolate. Pero NO decimos I am liking chocolate. Eso es incorrecto.

Exacto. Más ejemplos.
  • She loves Italian food – Ella ama la comida italiana. NO She is loving Italian food.
  • We prefer home-cooked meals – Preferimos comidas caseras. NO We are preferring home-cooked meals.

Otro grupo importante de verbos de estado son los verbos de perception – percepción. To see – ver, to hear – oír, to smell – oler, to taste – saber o tener sabor. Por ejemplo, This soup tastes delicious – Esta sopa sabe deliciosa. NO This soup is tasting delicious.

Pero cuidado, algunos verbos pueden ser de acción O de estado, dependiendo del significado. Por ejemplo, to think. Cuando significa have an opinion – tener una opinión, es verbo de estado. I think chocolate is the best – Pienso que el chocolate es lo mejor.

Pero cuando think significa the mental process – el proceso mental de pensar, es verbo de acción. I am thinking about what to cook for dinner – Estoy pensando en qué cocinar para la cena.

Otro ejemplo es to have. Cuando significa possess – poseer, es verbo de estado. I have a big kitchen – Tengo una cocina grande. Pero cuando significa to eat or drink, es verbo de acción. I am having lunch – Estoy almorzando.

Excelente. Entonces, para resumir, usamos presente simple para rutinas y hechos, y presente continuo para acciones en progreso. Y recordamos que los verbos de estado normalmente solo van en presente simple.

We live in a stressful world, and daily life can sometimes make us feel tired, stressed, or depressed. Some people go to the doctor for help, others try alternative therapies, but the place to find a cure could be somewhere completely different: in the kitchen.

Doctor Paul Clayton, a food expert from Middlesex University, says that what you eat is affected by what you eat and drink, just like every other part of your body. Certain types of food contain substances that affect how you think and feel.

For example, food that is high in carbohydrates can make us feel more relaxed. It also makes us feel happy. Chocolate has shown that people on diets often begin to feel a little depressed after two weeks because they are eating fewer carbohydrates.

On the other hand, food that is rich in protein makes us feel awake and focused. Eating protein has also shown that schoolchildren who eat a high-protein breakfast often do better at school than children whose breakfast is lower in protein. Also, eating the right kind of meal at lunchtime can make a difference if you have an exam in the afternoon or a business meeting where you need to make important decisions.

In an experiment for a television show, two chess players, both former champions, had different meals and then played a game of chess against each other. Paul had a plate of prosciutto and salad, full of protein from the red meat, and his opponent Terry ate a plate of pasta with creamy sauce, full of carbohydrates. In the chess match Terry felt sleepy and took much longer than Paul to make decisions. Paul made quick moves to take the game.

The experiment was repeated several times with the same result. Another way that mood food could become useful is for some medications doctors prescribe for stress. In a study, Swiss researchers discovered that eating two bars of dark chocolate candy bar, about four ounces, had beneficial effects for highly stressed people. Not only did eating the dark chocolate help reduce stress, it was also shown to improve mood and reduce high blood pressure.

Why does chocolate make people less stressed? First, it causes the body to reduce the level of stress hormones such as cortisol. Second, it reduces the fight or flight hormones, a hormone that makes people want to start a fight or run away when they feel very stressed. In addition, it contains other compounds that lower blood pressure and improve your mood. So, doing all these things, along with its delicious taste, make chocolate a powerful mood changer.

Doctor Clayton concludes that blueberries and cocoa can raise concentration levels for up to five hours. Also, food that is high in protein helps your brain to work more efficiently. For relaxation and to sleep better, eat carbohydrates. Dark green vegetables like cabbage and spinach, and oily fish like salmon, eaten regularly can help to fight depression.
