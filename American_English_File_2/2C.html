Orador 1: Hola a todos y bienvenidos al podcast "American English File 2 Podcast".
Hoy exploraremos una historia emocionante titulada "One dark October evening" o "Una oscura noche de octubre".

Orador 2: Así es.
Hoy hablaremos sobre los conectores de tiempo, frases verbales, acentuación de palabras, vocabulario, y también escucharemos una historia que nos ayudará a entender todo esto en contexto.

Orador 1: Empecemos con los conectores de tiempo.
Estas son palabras o frases que usamos para indicar el orden de los eventos.
Por ejemplo:

After that (Después de eso)
The next day (Al día siguiente)
One evening in October (Una noche de octubre)
Suddenly (De repente)
Two minutes later (Dos minutos después)
When (Cuando)
While (Mientras)

Orador 2: Exacto.
Estas expresiones nos ayudan a contar historias de forma clara y ordenada.
Ahora, escuchemos la historia que acompaña este tema.
Presten atención a los conectores de tiempo.

Orador 1: A continuación leeremos la lectura "One dark October evening".

Hannah met Jamie in the summer of 2010. It was Hannah's 21st birthday, and she and her friends went to a club. They wanted to dance, but they didn't like the music, so Hannah went to speak to the DJ. "This music is awful," she said. "Could you play something else?" The DJ looked at her and said, "Don't worry, I have the perfect song for you."

Two minutes later, he said, "The next song is by Scouting For Girls. It's called Blue As Your Eyes, and it's for a beautiful girl who's dancing over there." Hannah knew that the song was for her. When Hannah and her friends left the club, the DJ was waiting for her at the door. "Hi, I'm Jamie," he said to Hannah. "Can I see you again?" So Hannah gave him her phone number.

The next day, Jamie called Hannah and invited her to dinner. He took her to a very romantic restaurant, and they talked all evening. Although the food wasn't very good, they had a wonderful time. After that, Jamie and Hannah saw each other every day. Every evening when Hannah finished work, they met at 5:30 in a coffee shop on Bridge Street. They were madly in love.

One evening in October, Hannah was at work. As usual she was going to meet Jamie at 5:30. It was dark and it was raining. She looked at her watch. It was 5:20! She was going to be late! She ran to her car and got in. At 5:25 she was driving along Bridge Street.

She was going very fast because she was in a hurry. Suddenly, a man ran across the street. He was wearing a dark coat, so Hannah didn't see him at first. Quickly, she put her foot on the brake...

Vocabulario: 21st /ˈtwɛnti fɜːrst/ vigésimo primero; across /əˈkrɒs/ a través de; Although /ɔːlˈðoʊ/ aunque; awful /ˈɔːfəl/ horrible, terrible; Bridge /brɪdʒ/ puente; called /kɔːld/ llamó; coat /koʊt/ abrigo; Could /kʊd/ podría; dinner /ˈdɪnər/ cena; driving /ˈdraɪvɪŋ/ conduciendo; finished /ˈfɪnɪʃt/ terminó; gave /ɡeɪv/ dio; hurry /ˈhɜːri/ prisa, apurarse; invited /ɪnˈvaɪtɪd/ invitó; knew /njuː/ sabía, conocía; left /lɛft/ dejó, se fue; looked /lʊkt/ miró; madly /ˈmædli/ locamente; raining /ˈreɪnɪŋ/ lloviendo; ran /ræn/ corrió; said /sɛd/ dijo; saw /sɔː/ vio; something else /ˈsʌmθɪŋ ɛls/ algo más; took /tʊk/ tomó, llevó; wanted /ˈwɒntɪd/ quería, deseaba; wearing /ˈwɛərɪŋ/ llevando puesto; went /wɛnt/ fue.

Orador 2: Muy bien.
Ahora que escuchamos la historia, vamos a responder algunas preguntas para comprobar la comprensión.

Why did Hannah go and speak to Jamie? (¿Por qué fue Hannah a hablar con Jamie?)
Because she didn’t like the music and Jamie was the DJ. (Porque no le gustaba la música y Jamie era el DJ.)

Why did Jamie play Blue As Your Eyes? (¿Por qué Jamie tocó "Azul como tus ojos"?)
Because it was for Hannah. (Porque era para Hannah.)

What happened when Hannah left the club? (¿Qué pasó cuando Hannah salió del club?)
She and her friends went to a restaurant. (Ella y sus amigas fueron a un restaurante.)

What was the restaurant like? (¿Cómo era el restaurante?)
It was very romantic, but the food wasn’t very good. (Era muy romántico, pero la comida no era muy buena.)

Where did they go every evening after that? (¿A dónde iban todas las noches después de eso?)
They met at 5:30 in a coffee shop on Bridge Street. (Se encontraban a las 5:30 en una cafetería en Bridge Street.)

What was the weather like that evening? (¿Cómo estaba el clima esa noche?)
It was dark and raining. (Estaba oscuro y llovía.)

Why was Hannah driving fast? (¿Por qué Hannah iba manejando rápido?)
Because she was late. (Porque iba tarde.)

Why didn’t she see the man? (¿Por qué no vio al hombre?)
Because he was wearing a dark coat and it was raining. (Porque llevaba un abrigo oscuro y estaba lloviendo.)

Orador 1: Ahora vamos a practicar completando algunas oraciones con conectores como so (así que), because (porque) o although (aunque):

She was going very fast because she was in a hurry. (Iba muy rápido porque tenía prisa.)

Although the food wasn’t very good, they had a wonderful time. (Aunque la comida no era muy buena, pasaron un rato maravilloso.)

He was wearing a dark coat, so Hannah didn’t see him at first. (Llevaba un abrigo oscuro, así que Hannah no lo vio al principio.)

Orador 2: Continuamos con la pronunciación.
Hoy vamos a enfocarnos en el word stress o acento en las palabras de dos sílabas.
En inglés, la mayoría de las palabras de dos sílabas se acentúan en la primera sílaba.
Por ejemplo:

mother (madre)
birthday (cumpleaños)
invite (invitar)
perfect (perfecto)

Orador 1: Pero hay excepciones, especialmente con verbos o preposiciones.
Algunos ejemplos donde el acento está en la segunda sílaba:

arrive (llegar)
begin (empezar)
before (antes)

Orador 2: Ahora vamos al vocabulario.
Vamos a combinar verbos con frases para formar expresiones comunes que aparecieron en la historia.

Por ejemplo:
invite somebody to dinner (invitar a alguien a cenar)
drive along Bridge Street (conducir por la calle Bridge)
give somebody your phone number (darle tu número de teléfono a alguien)
play a song (tocar una canción)
run across the street (cruzar la calle corriendo)

Orador 1: Estas frases nos ayudan a describir acciones comunes en la vida diaria.
Ahora, intenta recordar los verbos que corresponden a estas frases sin mirar el texto.

Orador 2: Para practicar la expresión oral, trata de contar la historia con tus propias palabras.
Recuerda usar conectores de tiempo y frases verbales como las que hemos aprendido.
Por ejemplo: "One evening in October, Hannah went to a club with her friends. Suddenly, she talked to the DJ because she didn’t like the music. Two minutes later, he played a special song for her."
