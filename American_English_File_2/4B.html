Orador 1: ¡Hola a todos! Bienvenidos a un nuevo episodio de "American English File 2 Podcast". Hoy vamos a hablar sobre la moda y las compras, un tema que nos encanta.

Orador 2: Así es. Vamos a explorar vocabulario, gramática, pronunciación, lectura y ejercicios relacionados con este tema. ¡Comencemos!

Orador 1: Empezamos con una entrevista titulada "The Style Interview". Vamos a leerla a continuación.

THE STYLE INTERVIEW
LINDKA CIERACH is a fashion designer. She makes very exclusive clothes for women. She has made clothes for many celebrities including members of the British royal family, for example Kate Middleton and Sarah Ferguson, whose wedding dress she designed, and actresses like Helen Mirren.

DID YOU ALWAYS WANT TO BE A DESIGNER?
Not at all! When I was at school I had problems reading, and later I was diagnosed as dyslexic. After school I took a secretarial course and then I got a job at Vogue magazine. I loved it, and there I realized that what I wanted to do was design clothes.

WHY DO YOU THINK PEOPLE LIKE YOUR CLOTHES?
Being a female designer has many advantages. My customers leave the studio feeling like a million dollars!

WHAT NATIONALITY DO YOU THINK HAS THE BEST FASHION SENSE?
Probably the Italians.

HAVE YOU EVER BEEN TO A COSTUME PARTY?
I hate costume parties. But I can remember one, when I was ten.

WHAT DID YOU GO AS?
My mother made me and my younger sister dress as a bride and bridegroom - I was the bridegroom!

HAVE YOU EVER MET SOMEONE WHO WAS WEARING EXACTLY THE SAME OUTFIT AS YOU?
Never, thank goodness! I'm lucky because I can choose from a large selection of our collection each season!

HAVE YOU EVER FALLEN BECAUSE YOU WERE WEARING VERY HIGH HEELS?
I've never fallen, but once I had to take off my shoes in the middle of a reception at the House of Lords!

WHAT DID YOU DO?
I walked out into the street in bare feet and jumped into a taxi!

HAVE YOU EVER DESIGNED CLOTHES FOR A MAN?
Yes, I have.

WHEN WAS IT?
It was when I was studying at the London College of Fashion. I designed my first men's wear collection - shirts, pants, and leather jackets. He didn't need to buy any clothes that year!

Glossary
bride / bridegroom a woman/man on the day of her / his wedding
The House of Lords the second house of the British Parliament

Orador 2: Ahora que escuchamos la entrevista con Lindka Cierach, diseñadora de moda, vamos a comentar algunas de sus respuestas. Por ejemplo, cuando le preguntan:

DO YOU ALWAYS WANT TO BE A DESIGNER?
¿Siempre quisiste ser diseñadora?
Ella responde que no, que al principio quería ser actriz.

Orador 1: También menciona que diseñó su primera colección masculina cuando estudiaba en el London College of Fashion. Ella dice:
He didn’t need to buy any clothes that year!
¡Él no necesitó comprar ropa ese año!

Orador 2: Y sobre los tacones altos, ella dice:
I had to take off my shoes in the middle of a reception at the House of Lords!
¡Tuve que quitarme los zapatos en medio de una recepción en la Cámara de los Lores!

Orador 1: Ahora pasemos a la sección de gramática. Vamos a hablar del present perfect y el simple past. El present perfect se usa para hablar de experiencias en un tiempo no especificado en el pasado. Por ejemplo:
Have you ever designed clothes for a man?
¿Alguna vez has diseñado ropa para un hombre?

Orador 2: Ella responde:
Yes, I have.
Sí, lo he hecho.
Este es un ejemplo del present perfect. Se forma con el verbo auxiliar have o has más el participio pasado del verbo principal.

Orador 1: En cambio, el pasado simple se usa para hablar de momentos específicos en el pasado. Como cuando ella dice:
It was when I was studying at the London College of Fashion.
Fue cuando estudiaba en el London College of Fashion.

Orador 2: Ahora vamos con la sección de listening. Escucharemos el siguiente audio con cuatro personas respondiendo a la pregunta:
Have you ever bought something that you’ve never worn?
¿Alguna vez has comprado algo que nunca has usado?

1 Interviewer: Have you ever bought something that you've never worn?
A: Yes. Hasn't everyone? I remember some pants I bought that I never wore.
Interviewer: What was the problem with them?
A: They were very tight, black leather pants that I bought from a second-hand store when I was about 20 years old. I remember when I was in the fitting room I thought they looked amazing. I thought I looked like Jim Morrison from the Doors. But when I got home, in the cold light of day, I realized that I looked more like one of the women from Abba! That's why I never wore them.

2 Interviewer: Have you ever bought something that you've never worn?
A: Yes, a karate uniform. I decided that I wanted to do karate, and I signed up for lessons and bought the uniform and the orange belt but then I changed my mind and decided not to take the lessons.
Interviewer: Why not?
A: I was worried that someone would knock my teeth out.
Interviewer: Do you still have the uniform?
A: No, I sold it on eBay.

3 Interviewer: Have you ever bought something that you've never worn?
A: Sadly it happens to me a lot because I hate clothes shopping, and I never try things on. For example, I have a shirt in my closet now that I've never worn.
Interviewer: Why not?
A: Well, I bought it in a hurry a few months ago, and then I put it away in my closet. A few weeks later, I took it out and looked at it and I thought, "Why did I buy this?" It's awful - pink and purple stripes. And of course I didn't have the receipt, so I couldn't take it back.

4 Interviewer: Have you ever bought something that you've never worn?
A: A lot of things, actually. The last one was a brown leather coat.
Interviewer: What was wrong with it?
A: Well, I bought it online from a website that has cheap deals, but when it arrived, it looked completely different from what it looked like on the computer screen, and I decided I didn't like it. So it's in my closet. I'm sure I'm never going to wear it, but maybe I'll give it to someone as a present.

Orador 1: Después de escuchar, identificamos qué compraron y cuál fue el problema. Por ejemplo:
Bought something online, but didn’t like the clothes when they arrived.
Compró algo en línea, pero no le gustó la ropa cuando llegó.

Orador 2: Otro ejemplo fue:
Wanted to look like a famous singer, but looked like another.
Quería parecerse a una cantante famosa, pero terminó pareciendo otra.

Orador 1: Ahora pasemos al vocabulario relacionado con las compras. Escuchamos frases como:
I bought it online. - Lo compré en línea.
I hate clothes shopping. - Odio comprar ropa.
I never try things on. - Nunca me pruebo la ropa.
I couldn't take it back. - No podía devolverlo.

Orador 2: Continuamos con la pronunciación. Hoy aprendemos a diferenciar el sonido /k/ como en key y /s/ como en snake. Por ejemplo:

* customer se pronuncia con /k/
* change se pronuncia con el sonido /tʃ/
* chic tiene sonido /ʃ/, como en she

Orador 1: En la actividad de speaking, practicamos con preguntas usando el participio pasado. Escuchemos algunas:

Have you ever bought or sold anything on eBay? Did you get a good price?
¿Alguna vez has comprado o vendido algo en eBay? ¿Conseguiste un buen precio?

Have you ever bought something online and had a problem with it? What was it?
¿Alguna vez has comprado algo en línea y tuviste un problema? ¿Cuál fue?

Have you ever had an argument with a salesperson? What was it about?
¿Alguna vez discutiste con un vendedor? ¿Sobre qué fue?

Orador 2: Más ejemplos para practicar:

Have you ever tried to exchange something without the receipt? Were you successful?
¿Alguna vez intentaste cambiar algo sin el recibo? ¿Tuviste éxito?

Have you ever accidentally taken something from a store without paying? What did you take? What happened?
¿Alguna vez tomaste algo por accidente de una tienda sin pagar? ¿Qué tomaste? ¿Qué pasó?

Have you ever got to the supermarket checkout and then found you didn’t have enough money? What did you do?
¿Alguna vez llegaste a la caja del supermercado y descubriste que no tenías suficiente dinero? ¿Qué hiciste?

Orador 1: ¡Muy buenas preguntas para usar en clase o en una conversación real!
