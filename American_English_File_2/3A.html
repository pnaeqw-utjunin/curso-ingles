Orador 1: Hola y bienvenidos a un nuevo episodio de "American English File 2 Podcast". Hoy hablaremos de "Plans and dreams" o "Planes y sueños".

Orador 2: Así es. Exploraremos vocabulario relacionado con los aeropuertos, usaremos la estructura "be going to" para hablar de planes y predicciones, y también escucharemos a algunas personas hablar sobre sus sueños de viaje.

Orador 1: Empezamos con vocabulario. Cuando estás en un aeropuerto, ves muchos letreros. Algunas palabras comunes son:
Arrivals (Llegadas)
Departures (Salidas)
Check-in (Registro)
Gates (Puertas de embarque)
Baggage claim (Reclamo de equipaje)
Passport control (Control de pasaportes)

Orador 2: Vamos a escuchar a tres personas que están en un aeropuerto. Ellos son Olivia, Matthew y Lily. Cada uno tiene un plan diferente. Escuchemos cuál es su destino, por qué viajan, y otros detalles.

Orador 1: A continuación leeremos la descripción de los planes de Olivia, Matthew y Lily.

Orador 1: Planes de Olivia:
Interviewer: Excuse me, do you have a minute?
Olivia: Yes, sure.
Interviewer: Where are you going?
Olivia: To Nicaragua.
Interviewer: For a vacation?
Olivia: No, I’m going to do volunteer work. I’m going to teach English to young children.
Interviewer: Where exactly in Nicaragua are you going?
Olivia: To a town called Esteli. It’s about 93 miles from Managua.
Interviewer: How long are you going to be there for?
Olivia: I’m going to be in Esteli for six weeks, and after that I’m going to travel around Nicaragua for a month.
Interviewer: That sounds amazing.
Olivia: Yes, I'm really looking forward to it.
Interviewer: Are you feeling nervous at all?
Olivia: A little, because I don’t speak much Spanish. But they’re going to give us a 40-hour language course when we arrive, so I hope that’s enough.
Interviewer: Well good luck and have a great time.
Olivia: Thanks. I’m sure it’s going to be an amazing experience.

Orador 2: Planes de Matthew
Interviewer: Excuse me, do you have a minute?
Matthew: Yeah, OK.
Interviewer: Where are you going?
Matthew: To Australia.
Interviewer: That’s a long flight. Are you going to stop on the way?
Matthew: No, I’m going direct to Melbourne.
Interviewer: Why Melbourne?
Matthew: I’m going to work there. I’m a model and we’re going to do a photo shoot for a magazine.
Interviewer: That sounds exciting. What kind of clothes are you going to model?
Matthew: Winter clothes, for next season. It’s winter in Australia now, so it’s going to be pretty cold. That’s why we’re going there.
Interviewer: Of course, it’s their winter. How cold do you think it’s going to be?
Matthew: I’m not really sure. About 45 to 50 degrees Fahrenheit during the day and colder at night, I suppose.
Interviewer: Well, have a good trip, and I hope the photos are fabulous!
Matthew: Thanks.

Orador 1: Planes de Lily
Interviewer: Excuse me, do you have a minute?
Lily: OK, sure.
Interviewer: Where are you going?
Lily: To San Francisco.
Interviewer: Why are you going there?
Lily: I’m going to a conference.
Interviewer: So it’s a work trip.
Lily: Yes. But I’m also going to see an old friend there. Actually, an old boyfriend. Someone I went out with a long time ago.
Interviewer: When did you decide to meet up again?
Lily: Well, I knew he was working at the University of San Francisco, so when the conference came up about a month ago I got in touch with him on Facebook.
Interviewer: Is he going to meet you at the airport?
Lily: I don’t think so! But who knows?
Interviewer: How do you feel about it?
Lily: Pretty excited. It’s going to be strange meeting again after all these years.
Interviewer: Well, good luck. I’m sure you’re going to have a great time. And enjoy the conference, too.
Lily: Thank you very much.

Orador 2: Vamos a resumir lo que cada persona nos contó sobre sus planes de viaje, como si estuviéramos haciendo una pequeña ficha con los datos principales:

Olivia va a la India porque quiere enseñar inglés. Va a trabajar para una ONG.
Matthew va a Tailandia porque quiere ver a su ex-pareja. Allá va a hacer una sesión de fotos.
Lily va de vacaciones a Brasil. Quiere visitar un lugar exótico y relajarse.

Orador 1: Muy bien. Ahora pasemos a la gramática. Hoy vamos a estudiar el uso de "be going to" para hablar de planes y predicciones.

Orador 2: Por ejemplo:
I’m going to teach English. (Voy a enseñar inglés.) [Plan]
It’s going to rain. (Va a llover.) [Predicción]

Orador 1: La estructura es: sujeto + verbo to be + going to + verbo principal. Es muy útil y muy común al hablar del futuro.

Orador 2: Ahora escucha algunas frases y repítelas. Presta atención al ritmo y al acento:
What are you going to do tonight? (¿Qué vas a hacer esta noche?)
Are you going to see a movie? (¿Vas a ver una película?)
I’m going to cook a meal for you. (Voy a cocinar una comida para ti.)

Orador 1: Nota que cuando hablamos rápido, "going to" suena como gonna.
Por ejemplo: "What are you gonna do?"

Orador 2: Ahora leamos un artículo sobre los mejores aeropuertos del mundo. Se titula "Top airports in the world".

For many people, airports are a nightmare—long lines when you check in and go through security, and an even longer wait if your flight is delayed. But there are some airports where you can actually enjoy yourself. All good airports have excellent facilities for business people and children, free Wi-Fi, restaurants, cafes, and stores. But the best airports have much more...

SINGAPORE CHANGI AIRPORT
is paradise for flower lovers because it has an indoor orchid garden! It also has a rooftop swimming pool and a free sight-seeing tour for people who have at least five hours to wait for their connecting flight.

HONG KONG INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT
If you like computer games, you’ll never be bored here—there are dozens of free PlayStations all over the terminals! It’s also good for people with no sense of direction: "Airport Ambassadors" in red coats help you navigate.

INCHEON INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT
is the place to relax. You can visit the hair salon for beauty treatments or a massage. Sports fans can play golf at the airport’s 72-hole golf course!

MUNICH AIRPORT
keeps passengers entertained with a 60-seat movie theater showing non-stop films. Free coffee and tea are available near all seating areas, along with magazines and newspapers.

OSAKA INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT (Japan)
Ideal for health-conscious travelers: it has dentist and doctor offices onsite. For those with animals, there’s even a pet hotel.

VANCOUVER INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT
If your busy schedule leaves no time for laundry, drop it off at the airport cleaners before boarding. Pick up your clean clothes when you return!

Orador 1: Algunos aeropuertos son verdaderamente increíbles. Por ejemplo:
Singapore Changi Airport tiene jardines, una piscina en el techo, y cines. (Singapore Changi Airport has gardens, a rooftop pool, and cinemas.)
Munich Airport tiene un cine de 60 asientos y zonas de juegos. (Munich Airport has a 60-seat movie theater and game areas.)
Vancouver Airport tiene un servicio para lavar tu ropa. (Vancouver Airport has a laundry drop-off service.)
