Orador 1: Hola a todos y bienvenidos a otro episodio de "American English File 2 Podcast". Soy Emily.

Orador 2: Y yo soy Carlos. Hoy vamos a hablar de un tema muy interesante: los padres y los adolescentes, "Parents and teenagers".

Orador 1: Comenzamos con una lectura sobre hábitos molestos entre adolescentes y sus padres.
Orador 2: A continuación leeremos la lectura "Teenagers have annoying habits – but so do their parents!".

Teenagers have annoying habits - but so do their parents!

Josh Lopez
They come into my room without knocking and then are surprised to see things they don't really want to know about.

Rachel Black
They just keep on texting when I'm telling them something really important and they say, "Yeah, yeah I heard you." Of course they didn't.

They always pick up the remote and change the channel when I'm watching something really interesting.

They leave their room in a terrible mess and then roll their eyes when I ask them to clean it.

Orador 1: Ahora vamos a comentar algunas frases de esta lectura. Por ejemplo:
They come into my room without knocking. - Ellos entran a mi habitación sin tocar la puerta.
They just keep texting. - Ellos siguen enviando mensajes de texto.
They tell me to do the dishes and then complain. - Me dicen que lave los platos y luego se quejan.

Orador 2: Vamos a reflexionar, ¿cuáles de estos hábitos te molestan a ti? Puedes comparar con un compañero y decir si estás de acuerdo o no.

Orador 1: Pasamos al vocabulario. Hoy tenemos tres verbos importantes relacionados con las tareas del hogar: housework, make y do.
Orador 2: Housework significa tareas del hogar. Por ejemplo, do the dishes - lavar los platos, do the laundry - lavar la ropa.
Orador 1: Make se usa con cosas como make the bed - tender la cama, o make dinner - preparar la cena.

Orador 2: Ahora vamos con la gramática: el presente perfecto con yet y already.
Orador 1: Yet se usa para preguntar o decir que algo aún no ha pasado. Por ejemplo:
Have you cleaned your room yet? - ¿Ya limpiaste tu cuarto?
Orador 2: Already se usa para decir que algo ya pasó. Por ejemplo:
I’ve already cleaned it. - Ya lo he limpiado.

Orador 1: En los ejercicios de gramática, veamos este diálogo:
Have you seen my yellow sweater? - ¿Has visto mi suéter amarillo?
I can’t find it. - No lo puedo encontrar.
Oh yes, I remember now. I borrowed it. - Oh sí, ahora recuerdo. Lo tomé prestado.

Orador 2: Otro ejemplo:
Why aren’t you doing your homework? - ¿Por qué no estás haciendo tu tarea?
I’ve already done it. - Ya la hice.

Orador 1: Escuchemos otros ejemplos con participios pasados:
I haven’t seen it. - No lo he visto.
He’s just cleaned the floor. - Él acaba de limpiar el piso.

Orador 2: Pasamos ahora a pronunciación. Vamos a practicar los sonidos /j/ como en yacht - yate, y /dʒ/ como en jazz - jazz.
Orador 1: Escucha y repite: yellow - amarillo, young - joven, joke - chiste, jazz - jazz, bridge - puente.

Orador 2: Ahora escuchemos y practiquemos estas oraciones:
Jim has joined a judo class. - Jim se ha unido a una clase de judo.
Have you worn your new uniform yet? - ¿Ya has usado tu nuevo uniforme?

Orador 1: Continuamos con la parte de escucha.
Orador 2: Escucharemos un programa de radio sobre adolescentes que cuidan a sus familiares.
Orador 1: Escuchemos:

Teenagers today have a bad reputation. People say that they are lazy and messy and that they do very little to help their parents around the house. But there are some teenagers for whom this description is just not true at all.

It is estimated that there are more than 1.4 million teenagers in the US who have to look after a member of their family, their mother or father or brother or sister. In many cases these young helpers, or "caregivers" as they are called, are doing things like feeding, washing, and taking care of family members, as well as doing their school work.

Orador 2: Ahora vamos a responder algunas preguntas:

1. What reputation do teenagers have? - ¿Qué reputación tienen los adolescentes?
2. How many teenagers have to look after a family member? - ¿Cuántos adolescentes cuidan a un miembro de la familia?
3. What kinds of responsibilities do these young helpers have? - ¿Qué responsabilidades tienen estos jóvenes?

Orador 1: En la segunda parte del audio escucharemos a dos adolescentes, Alice y Daniel, hablando sobre sus vidas.
Orador 2: Escuchemos:

Host: I'd like to welcome to the program two of these teenagers, Alice and Daniel, who are 17 years old, and who both take care of family members. Hello, Alice, hello Daniel.

Alice / Daniel: Hi.

Host: Who do you take care of?

Alice: I take care of my mom. She has M.E. – it's a disease – it means that she feels tired all the time and she can't walk very well. And I also take care of my younger brother and sister. He's six and she's four.

Daniel: I take care of my mom, too. She had a bad car accident seven years ago and she can't walk. I also take care of my little sister.

Host: You both do a lot of housework. What exactly do you do?

Alice: On a normal day, I get up early and I clean the house and I do the ironing. After school I sometimes take my mom to the store in her wheelchair. In the evening my dad makes the dinner – I'm not very good at cooking! But I make sure my brother and sister eat their dinner and then I put them to bed.

Daniel: My day's pretty similar. I clean the house and iron but I also do the cooking and the grocery shopping. My dad left home four years ago, so we're on our own. I take my sister to school and make sure that my mom is OK. I have to give her massages every evening.

Host: How do you feel about the way you live?

Alice: I don't really mind taking care of my mom. She's sick and she needs my help. But sometimes I feel a little sad when I can't go out because there are things to do around the house. And I sometimes get angry with my school friends. They don't really understand the problems I have at home. All they think about is clothes, boys, and going out.

Daniel: I like what I do because I'm helping my mom, and I'm helping my sister at the same time. Of course it's true that I can't go out much because I need to spend most of my time at home. I sometimes go out with my friends, but I don't like leaving my mom by herself. I always make sure that I have my cell phone. If my mom needs anything, she calls me and I go back home. It's not a problem for me. It's just part of my life.

Host: You're both doing a great job. Thanks very much for coming on the program.

Orador 1: Vamos a reflexionar: ¿De qué manera los adolescentes hablan de sus vidas? ¿Se sienten positivos o negativos?
