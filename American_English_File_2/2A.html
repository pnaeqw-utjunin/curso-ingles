Orador 1: Hola a todos y bienvenidos al "American English File 2 Podcast".
En este episodio hablaremos sobre el tema "Right place, wrong person" o "El lugar correcto, la persona equivocada".

Orador 2: Hoy aprenderemos vocabulario relacionado con las vacaciones, repasaremos el pasado simple con verbos regulares e irregulares, y escucharemos las experiencias de Joe y Laura durante sus vacaciones.

Orador 1: Empecemos con el vocabulario.
¿Qué cosas disfrutas hacer cuando estás de vacaciones?
Aquí tienes algunos ejemplos:

relaxing (relajarse)
going to museums (ir a museos)
going to the beach (ir a la playa)
staying in a hotel (quedarse en un hotel)
going sightseeing (hacer turismo)
going with friends (ir con amigos)

Orador 2: También es común hablar sobre tus vacaciones ideales.
Por ejemplo:
"My perfect summer vacation would be going abroad with my family." (Mis vacaciones de verano perfectas serían ir al extranjero con mi familia.)

Orador 1: Ahora escuchemos la historia de Joe, un asistente de vuelo que fue de vacaciones a Tailandia con su novia.

Joe, 28, a flight attendant

Last October, I went on vacation to Thailand for two weeks with my girlfriend, Mia.

The vacation began well. We spent two days in Bangkok and saw the Floating Market and the Royal Palace. But things went wrong when we left Bangkok. I wanted to stay in hostels, which were basic but clean, but Mia said they were too uncomfortable. So we stayed in very expensive hotels. I wanted to experience the local atmosphere, but Mia just wanted to go shopping. I thought I knew Mia well, but you don't know a person until you travel with him or her. It was awful! We argued about everything.

For our last four days we went to Ko Chang, a beautiful island. It was like being in paradise. The weather was beautiful and the beaches were wonderful, but we just sunbathed without speaking. We spent our last night back in Bangkok, and we met some travelers from Australia. They were really friendly, and Mia started flirting with one of the boys. That was the end.

When we arrived at O'Hare International Airport the next day, we decided to break up.

I took hundreds of photos, but when I got home I didn't show them to anyone.

you don't know a person until you travel with him or her

Vocabulary: anyone /ˈen.i.wʌn/ nadie / cualquiera; argued /ˈɑːɡ.juːd/ discutimos; arrived /əˈraɪvd/ llegamos; began /bɪˈɡæn/ comenzó; being /ˈbiː.ɪŋ/ siendo; break up /ˈbreɪk ʌp/ terminar / romper; flirting /ˈflɜː.tɪŋ/ coqueteando; got /ɡɒt/ llegué / obtuve; knew /njuː/ sabía / conocía; know /nəʊ/ conocer / saber; left /lɛft/ dejamos / salimos de; met /mɛt/ conocimos; said /sɛd/ dijo; saw /sɔː/ vi; spent /spɛnt/ pasé; stayed /steɪd/ nos quedamos; sunbathed /ˈsʌn.beɪðd/ tomamos el sol; thought /θɔːt/ pensé; took /tʊk/ tomé; wanted /ˈwɒn.tɪd/ quería; was /wɒz/ fue / era; went /wɛnt/ fui; went wrong /wɛnt rɒŋ/ salió mal; Orador 2: Vamos con algunas preguntas para comprobar la comprensión:

Where did Joe go on vacation? (¿Adónde fue Joe de vacaciones?)
He went to Thailand. (Fue a Tailandia.)

Who did he go with? (¿Con quién fue?)
He went with his girlfriend, Mia. (Fue con su novia, Mia.)

Where did he stay? (¿Dónde se quedó?)
He stayed in basic hotels in Bangkok. (Se quedó en hoteles sencillos en Bangkok.)

What was the weather like? (¿Cómo estaba el clima?)
It was hot and sunny. (Estaba caluroso y soleado.)

Why didn’t he enjoy the vacation? (¿Por qué no disfrutó las vacaciones?)
Because he argued with Mia and they broke up. (Porque discutió con Mia y terminaron.)

Orador 1: Ahora conozcamos la historia de Laura, una enfermera que también fue a Costa Rica con su mejor amiga.

Orador 1: A continuación leeremos la lectura "Laura’s Vacation".

Laura, 26, a nurse

Last spring, my best friend Isabelle and I booked a vacation in Costa Rica. We rented a small house for a week with a fantastic view of the ocean. At the last minute another friend, Linda, asked if she could come, too. We felt sorry for her because she had problems with her boyfriend, so we said yes.

Costa Rica was magical and the weather was perfect, but the vacation was a disaster for one simple reason: Linda was so cheap! She has a good job so she's not poor, but she just didn't want to pay for anything. When we went sightseeing, she didn't want to go on any cruises or do any nature activities that cost money. When we went on a zipline tour, she complained that it was too expensive. When we went to have lunch or dinner, she always wanted to go to cheap restaurants or she bought a sandwich and ate it in the house. But the night I invited her and Isabelle out on my birthday, she chose the most expensive things on the menu! The worst thing was that although Isabelle and I paid for the house, Linda never once bought us a coffee or a snack.

I'd love to go back to Costa Rica one day... but without Linda.

Vocabulary: although /ɔːlˈðoʊ/ aunque; asked /æskt/ preguntó; ate /eɪt/ comió; booked /bʊkt/ reservé; bought /bɔːt/ compró; cheap /tʃiːp/ barato; complained /kəmˈpleɪnd/ se quejó; could /kʊd/ podía; cruises /kruːzɪz/ cruceros; felt /fɛlt/ sentimos; had /hæd/ tenía; invited /ɪnˈvaɪtɪd/ invitó; paid /peɪd/ pagó; poor /pʊər/ pobre; reason /ˈriːzən/ razón; rented /ˈrɛntɪd/ alquilamos; said /sɛd/ dijimos; sightseeing /ˈsaɪtˌsiːɪŋ/ turismo; want /wɒnt/ querer; wanted /ˈwɒn.tɪd/ quería; was /wɒz/ fue / estaba; weather /ˈwɛðər/ clima; went /wɛnt/ fuimos; worst /wɜːst/ peor; zipline tour /ˈzɪp.laɪn tʊr/ tour de tirolesa; Orador 2: Respondamos algunas preguntas sobre su experiencia:

Where did Laura go on vacation? (¿Adónde fue Laura de vacaciones?)
She went to Costa Rica. (Fue a Costa Rica.)

Who did she go with? (¿Con quién fue?)
She went with her best friend, Isabel. (Fue con su mejor amiga, Isabel.)

Why was the vacation difficult? (¿Por qué fue difícil la vacación?)
Because her friend was cheap and they didn’t enjoy the same things. (Porque su amiga era tacaña y no disfrutaban las mismas cosas.)

Orador 1: En ambas historias, el lugar era perfecto pero las personas con quienes viajaron no eran las adecuadas.
Por eso el título: "Right place, wrong person" (El lugar correcto, la persona equivocada).

Orador 2: Continuemos con gramática.
Hoy hablaremos sobre el pasado simple.
Algunos verbos son regulares y solo agregamos -ed, como:

arrive → arrived (llegar → llegó)
want → wanted (querer → quiso)
stay → stayed (quedarse → se quedó)

Orador 1: Otros son irregulares y cambian completamente.
Por ejemplo:

go → went (ir → fue)
have → had (tener → tuvo)
take → took (tomar → tomó)
argue → argued (discutir → discutió)
break up → broke up (terminar una relación → terminó)

Orador 2: Vamos a buscar dos verbos en pasado en los textos y reconocer si son regulares o irregulares.

Orador 1: Ahora pasemos a la pronunciación.
El pasado de los verbos regulares termina en -ed, pero se pronuncia de tres formas distintas:

/t/ como en "walked" (caminó)
/d/ como en "played" (jugó)
/ɪd/ como en "wanted" (quiso)

Orador 2: Presta atención al sonido al final del verbo.
No siempre suena como se escribe.

Orador 1: Para practicar el speaking, piensa en tus últimas vacaciones.
¿Adónde fuiste?
¿Con quién?
¿Qué hiciste?
¿Qué fue lo mejor o lo peor?

Orador 2: Aquí tienes un ejemplo:
"Last year I went to the beach with my cousins. We stayed in a small house. It was sunny and we swam every day. I had a great time!" (El año pasado fui a la playa con mis primos. Nos quedamos en una casa pequeña. Hacía sol y nadamos todos los días. ¡La pasé genial!)
