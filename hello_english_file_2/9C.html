Escucha esta historia en inglés. Recuerda que no traduciremos todo, pero presta atención porque contiene muchos ejemplos de la gramática del día.

"Daniel Torres was born in Mexico City in 1984. His parents were music teachers, so Daniel grew up surrounded by instruments. There were guitars, pianos, violins, and drums everywhere in his house. When he was six years old, he joined a children's choir, and his teachers immediately noticed that he had a very special voice.

Daniel spent his teenage years performing in school concerts and local festivals. At the age of seventeen, he wrote his first original song, which later became one of his most popular hits. After graduating from high school, he moved to Monterrey to study music production. Life there was not easy, but he never stopped singing.

In 2006, Daniel released his first album. The critics loved it, and people began to recognize his voice on the radio. Since then, he has recorded six studio albums, collaborated with many artists, and won several national awards. Daniel says that he has always felt connected to music, even during difficult times.

Today, he continues touring across Latin America. Fans say he has one of the warmest voices in the region. Daniel often tells young musicians, 'Follow your passion. I've worked hard for many years, and music has given me more than I ever imagined.' He truly believes he was born to sing."

Qué historia tan inspiradora. Y está llena de ejemplos que nos ayudarán a entender la diferencia entre present perfect y simple past, así que vayamos a ello.

Ahora pasemos a la parte gramatical. La pregunta clave de hoy es: ¿cuándo usamos simple past y cuándo usamos present perfect?

El simple past se usa para acciones completadas en un momento definido del pasado. Por ejemplo:

He was born in 1984. – Él nació en 1984.

He moved to Monterrey. – Él se mudó a Monterrey.

He released his first album in 2006. – Él lanzó su primer álbum en 2006.

Observa que mencionamos un año, un evento específico. Eso es típico del simple past.

En cambio, el present perfect se utiliza para acciones que comenzaron en el pasado y continúan siendo importantes en el presente o cuya fecha no es relevante. Por ejemplo:

He has recorded six studio albums. – Él ha grabado seis álbumes de estudio.

He has collaborated with many artists. – Ha colaborado con muchos artistas.

He has always felt connected to music. – Siempre se ha sentido conectado con la música.

Lo clave es que hablamos de experiencias o logros acumulados a lo largo de la vida.

Y recuerda: si hay una fecha exacta, usa simple past. Si hablamos de experiencias sin precisar cuándo, usa present perfect.

Pasemos ahora a una pequeña práctica auditiva. Escucha estas frases y presta atención a cómo se usan los tiempos verbales.

"He started singing when he was six years old."

"He has sung in many countries."

"He released his first hit when he was seventeen."

"He has become one of the most popular singers in his region."

¿Ves? Unas frases hablan de momentos concretos y otras de experiencias acumuladas.

Vamos a trabajar ahora un poquito la pronunciación. Hoy practicaremos el sonido /ɔːr/, como en la palabra "born" – "nacido". Repite conmigo:

Recuerda alargar un poquito la vocal. Es un sonido más redondeado, no debes pronunciarlo como una "o" corta del español.

Sigamos con una práctica de conversación en formato podcast. Te daré ejemplos completos de cómo hablar de la vida de una persona famosa.

Por ejemplo:

"He was born in a small town." – "Él nació en un pueblo pequeño."

"He grew up in a musical family." – "Él creció en una familia musical."

"He has performed in big theaters." – "Él ha actuado en grandes teatros."

"She has won many awards." – "Ella ha ganado muchos premios."

"He started his career when he was young." – "Él comenzó su carrera cuando era joven."

"She has traveled around the world for her concerts." – "Ella ha viajado por el mundo para sus conciertos."

Estos ejemplos te muestran cómo combinar pasado simple con present perfect de manera natural.

Y ahora transformemos esto en práctica escrita pero adaptada al podcast, sin pedirte que completes nada. Solo escucha cómo podrías describir la vida de alguien.

Por ejemplo:

"My grandmother was born in 1948. She grew up in a rural area and went to school in a small town. She has worked as a teacher for more than thirty years and has helped hundreds of students. She retired recently, and she says she has enjoyed every day of her career."

O un ejemplo con una persona famosa:

"María López was born in Bogotá. She became famous after her first movie. Since then, she has acted in more than twenty films and has received several awards for her talent."

Lo importante es escuchar estructuras completas para que tu mente las incorpore sin necesidad de ejercicios de completar.
