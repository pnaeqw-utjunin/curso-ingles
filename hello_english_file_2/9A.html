¡Bienvenidos a otro episodio de Hello English!, el podcast donde aprender inglés es tan emocionante como enfrentar un cocodrilo… pero muchísimo más seguro. Hoy tenemos una clase especial titulada *What would you do?* – *¿Qué harías tú?* Nuestro objetivo es aprender a usar el segundo condicional, practicar vocabulario de animales y situaciones peligrosas, mejorar la pronunciación del acento de palabra, escuchar escenarios sorprendentes, leer una historia llena de adrenalina y también hablar y escribir en inglés con más naturalidad.

Exacto. Y para arrancar necesitamos vocabulario relacionado con animales y situaciones que pueden ponerte en aprietos. Empecemos:

  • Shark – tiburón.
  • Snake – serpiente.
  • Bull – toro.
  • Jellyfish – medusa.
  • Bear – oso.
  • Dog – perro.
  • Insect – insecto.
  • Sting – picadura.
  • Run away – huir.
  • Attack – atacar.
  • Dangerous – peligroso.
  • Calm – calma.
  • Bite – morder.
  • Breath slowly – respirar lentamente.
  • Climb – trepar.
  • Shout – gritar.
  • Move backward – moverse hacia atrás.

Perfecto. Y ahora que ya tenemos vocabulario, pasemos a una historia en inglés que nos ayudará a escuchar cómo funciona el segundo condicional en una situación imaginaria. Recuerden que esta lectura no se traduce completamente, solo escuchen y disfruten.

Lucas loved exploring nature. One afternoon, he decided to walk alone through a forest near his town. The air was fresh, and the sunlight filtered beautifully between the trees. But suddenly he stopped. Not far from him, a huge bear appeared behind a fallen log. Lucas froze. He knew that if he ran quickly, the bear would chase him. If he screamed loudly, the bear might feel threatened. He tried to think calmly.

“I read once,” Lucas whispered to himself, “that if you move slowly and avoid eye contact, the animal will feel less scared.” So he stepped backward carefully. The bear looked at him, curious but not aggressive. Lucas kept breathing slowly. If he had been careless, the situation would have been much worse.

Just when he thought everything was going well, he heard another noise. From the bushes on his right, a snake appeared, sliding toward him. Lucas thought, “Perfect. Now two animals want to meet me.” He smiled nervously. If he stepped on the snake, it would definitely bite him. If he stayed still, the bear might approach. He had to choose.

Finally, he climbed a small rock behind him. The snake passed below him and disappeared. The bear seemed bored and walked away. Lucas sighed in relief. Later he said to his friends, “If I hadn’t remembered those nature rules, I would have been in serious danger. If you were in my place, what would you do?”

¡Qué historia! Y lo mejor es que está repleta del segundo condicional. Así que pasemos a explicarlo.

El segundo condicional se usa para hablar de situaciones hipotéticas, irreales o poco probables. La estructura es: *If + past simple, would + verbo base.*

Ejemplos:
  • If I saw a bear, I would run away – Si yo viera un oso, huiría.
  • If you were in danger, you would call for help – Si estuvieras en peligro, pedirías ayuda.
  • If we met a shark, we would stay calm – Si encontráramos un tiburón, permaneceríamos calmados.

Y también podemos invertir el orden: *I would run away if I saw a bear.* – *Huiría si viera un oso.*

Otra cosa importante: en el segundo condicional usamos “were” para todas las personas.

Ejemplo:
*If I were you, I would stay quiet* – *Si yo fuera tú, me quedaría callado.*

Ahora algunos ejemplos divertidos para fijarlo:
  • If a jellyfish stung me, I would look for vinegar – Si una medusa me picara, buscaría vinagre.
  • If a bull ran toward me, I would move backward slowly – Si un toro corriera hacia mí, me movería hacia atrás lentamente.
  • If a shark appeared near me, I would swim calmly to the shore – Si un tiburón apareciera cerca de mí, nadaría con calma hacia la orilla.

¡Muy bien! Pasemos ahora a practicar pronunciación, especialmente el acento de palabra. Muchas palabras relacionadas con animales tienen acentos diferentes a los de otros idiomas. Escuchen:

  • Elephant – acento en E-le-phant.
  • Dolphin – DOL-phin.
  • Giraffe – gi-RAFFE.
  • Kangaroo – kan-ga-ROO.
  • Mosquito – mos-QUI-to.
  • Shark, lion, tiger… todas con acentos naturales muy marcados.

Escuchen estas frases con acento claro:
  • The DOLphin would help you if you were in the water.
  • If a mosQUIto bit me, I would use spray.
  • If a kan-ga-ROO ran toward me, I would jump away.

Ahora pasemos a una sección de escucha imaginada. Preparé unos escenarios para que los oyentes imaginen qué harían.

  • If you were swimming in the ocean and a jellyfish stung you… what would you do?
  • If a dog started running toward you angrily… what would you do?
  • If a snake appeared next to your foot… what would you do?
  • If you saw a huge shark while you were diving… what would you do?

Cada situación usa el segundo condicional y activa tu imaginación.

Y ahora practicaremos conversación con ejemplos naturales.

Yo digo: *If I saw a mouse in my kitchen, I would scream.* – *Si viera un ratón en mi cocina, gritaría.*
Y tú dices: *If I saw a mouse, I would pick it up carefully and take it outside.* – *Si yo viera uno, lo recogería cuidadosamente y lo sacaría afuera.*

Luego yo digo: *If a bear walked near me, I would freeze.* – *Si un oso caminara cerca de mí, me quedaría inmóvil.*
Y tú respondes: *If a bear walked near me, I would pretend to be a tree.* – *Si un oso caminara cerca de mí, fingiría ser un árbol.*

Me encanta fingir ser un árbol, siempre funciona en mi imaginación. Continuemos.

Ejemplos adicionales para practicar:
  • If a bull ran toward me, I would shout and wave my arms.
  • If a shark swam near me, I would stay calm.
  • If a bee landed on my hand, I would not move.
  • If a bird flew into my house, I would open all the windows.

¡Muy bien! Y ahora pasemos a la parte de escritura, pero contada oralmente.

Un oyente nos escribe: “¿Cómo puedo escribir sobre situaciones hipotéticas de forma natural?”

Respondemos:

*If you wanted to give advice, you could say: “If I were in your place, I would stay calm.”*

Otro ejemplo:

*If you were telling a story, you could say: “If the animal attacked, we would climb a tree.”*

Y uno más:

*If your character were afraid, you could write: “If I heard that noise, I would run away immediately.”*

Otro oyente nos pregunta: “¿Cómo puedo expresar decisiones difíciles?”

Respondemos:

  • If you needed to choose between danger and fear, you would explain your feelings.
  • If your friend were with you, you would decide together.
  • If the situation were too risky, you would leave immediately.

Para ir cerrando, repasemos lo aprendido. Hoy vimos el segundo condicional, practicamos vocabulario de animales peligrosos, trabajamos el acento de palabra, escuchamos escenarios extremos, leímos una historia sobre decisiones difíciles y practicamos cómo hablar y escribir hipótesis.

Y recuerda:

  • If you practiced every day, you would improve faster. – Si practicaras todos los días, mejorarías más rápido.
  • If you listened to Hello English! more often, you would speak confidently. – Si escucharas Hello English! más seguido, hablarías con confianza.
